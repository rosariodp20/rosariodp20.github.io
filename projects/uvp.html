Unisa Validation Platform

Software Engineering project

Unisa Validation Platform was developed as a group project for the Software Engineering course during my Bachelor's degree. The objective of the course was to enhance an existing project called English Validation, which aimed to provide a digitally automated process to assist the Department of Computer Science's administration in validating English language certificates.

Class Diagram of the reengineered project

With the newly implemented features, the system not only handled the validation of English language certificates but also automated the entire internship validation process. Each student could register if they had not already done so and then log into the system. After logging in, they could check the status of their requests, if any, or submit a new one. Regarding the validation of English certificates, the administration could correct potential typos made by students, verify the certificate's validity, and ultimately approve or reject the request. As for internship requests, the administration, companies, and professors could approve or reject an application, while the system administrator had the final authority to validate or deny the internship

Activity Diagram of the proposed system

Throughout the project, we were guided by three Master’s students who acted as Project Managers. Our goal as Bachelor's students was to follow all phases of the software development process using the waterfall model. We started with requirement analysis, followed by system design, then moved on to development, and finally conducted testing. The professor took on the role of Product Owner, responsible for evaluating and approving the final product. Since we successfully met all the predefined requirements, we passed the course without any issues.
