Visual Assistant

Software Project Management project

Visual Assistant was developed as a project for the Software Project Management course. The assignment required a team of eight undergraduate students to develop an application under the management of myself and a colleague. The professor took on the role of Product Owner, responsible for evaluating and approving the final project.

We structured the project using a hybrid development methodology, combining elements of SCRUM and the waterfall model. While we followed the entire software development lifecycle—from requirements analysis to testing—we also incorporated regular kickoff meetings and weekly evaluation sessions to monitor the project's progress.

For the application development, we deliberately selected technologies unfamiliar to the team members to challenge their learning curve. To facilitate this, I conducted an introductory training on Flutter and Dart, along with the integration of pre-trained Machine Learning models.

Example of object recognition obtained with Visual Assistant

The goal of Visual Assistant was to enhance the quality of life for visually impaired individuals. The application was designed to recognize people, animals, objects, and potential hazards in real-time using the smartphone camera while providing audio guidance to help users navigate a preselected route.

All documentation produced for project management

As Project Managers, our responsibilities extended beyond team coordination to include preparing all required documentation for the Product Owner. This involved reports and documentation on Cost Management, Quality Management, Risk Management, and other key project processes.
